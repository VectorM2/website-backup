Step-By-Step Project Development: What Do You Pay A Designer For?

At the first stage of developing a design project, it is necessary to establish a trusting relationship. The designer must gather all the necessary information, both technically and personally: your family, hobbies, wishes, preferences and must work carefully to reflect all these nuances in your interior. Interior design starts with this, and in order to bring the project to an ideal conclusion, the designer must be a good artist, a good manager, a talented organizer and, to a large extent, a psychologist.

A project starts with a plan and even more so with interior design and renovation. And the designer needs a lot to plan and reflect on paper:

Measurement of premises: It contains information on the plan of the dimensions of each room, the exact areas of all rooms, ceiling heights, distances between building elements (window and door openings, niches, ledges, etc. The designer needs this information for a detailed study of subsequent plans, calculating the amount of repair materials, lighting scenario of buildings. If redevelopment is planned, the designer will need to draw up another work plan of how the apartment will look after the walls and partitions are demolished or erected.

Electrical plan: After the designer and his client have decided on the location of the furniture, the designer has to prepare an electrical installation project. It involves planning the lighting scenario in the apartment, in other words the location of the lamps in the rooms. In addition, the electrical plan also provides the wiring diagram of various electrical appliances, as well as the location of the switches.
